Personal Projects

Simulation Study of Epidemic Spread in a 2D Environment

The objective of this study was to simulate the spread of an epidemic in a 2D environment. We employed an SIR (Susceptible-Infectious-Recovered) model-based approach to model the states of each individual and their interactions.

Methodology

We used the R programming language to develop the simulation code. The code was organized into several functions describing individuals' behaviors, movement, state updates, and interactions. We also utilized libraries such as "ggplot2" and "gridExtra" for generating charts and visualizing results.

The simulation ran in iterations over several days. At each iteration, we updated individuals' states based on their current state, neighborhood, and specified rules. Individuals could be susceptible, infectious, recovered, or deceased, and their state evolved based on probabilities of transmission, recovery, death, and return to susceptibility.

Average distances between individuals and certain centers were taken into account to influence interactions. Completed last year as part of assisting a first-year student.
